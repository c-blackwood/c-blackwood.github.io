California Housing Prices Prediction

Predicting housing value in California based on a variety of factors.

See our work below

What does the project do?

The dataset we found on Kaggle contains 20,000 entries from a census about housing prices during the 1990's. It contains the information of the location, median house age, total rooms, and the median house value. Our goal is to find patterns among these features and predict California housing prices.

Exploration


Exploration of data: Correlation table heatmap

First, we created a correlation table to see how different features correlated with the housing prices in California. This heatmap shows the correlation between all of our factors. The darker the square, the more the factors relate to each other. For example, the heatmap is darkest when two of the same factors are being compared. Total bedrooms and total rooms relate to each other because it can be assumed that if there are more bedrooms in a house, there are more rooms in total compared to houses with few bedrooms. Similarly, we can see that the squares are lighter for comparing total rooms and housing age. This tells us that the age of the house does not affect how many rooms it has!


Exploration of data: Pie Chart

Pie charts clearly demonstrate to us the part to whole relationship, showing us the percentage of how close each house is to the ocean. From this graph we can infer that majority of cities, where the majority of homes are located, are more than 1h or farther from the ocean.



Exploration of data: Scatterplot

Scatterplots graphs 3 sets of numerical data, with one variable on each axis, to look for a relationship between them. This Scatter Plot shows us the age of each of our 20,000 houses along California. From this Graph we can infer that majority of the house are older in San Jose and Cities near there, and from that we can conclude that cities like San Jose are older than those such as Redding.



Exploration of data: Histogram

A histogram is a bar graph-like representation of data that buckets a range of classes into columns along the horizontal x-axis. The vertical y-axis represents the number count or percentage of occurrences in the data for each column.This Histogram shows us how much each of our 20,000 houses in California is worth. As we can see the majority of the houses are between 65,000k to 190,000k. A higher bar means more houses. Since the bars are higher between 65,000k and 190,000k, we can deduce that the most common value of a house in California is about 150,000. The bars are lower between 300,000k and 475,000k, which tells us that the least common housing value in California is about 400,000l. Strangely, the housing value skyrockets at 500,000!

Machine Learning


Linear Regression: Explanation

Linear Regression is a supervised machine learning model that finds the best fit linear line between the independent and dependent variable.

Linear Regression: Results

Here we have a confusion matrix with the results of the linear regression applied on our testing dataset. A confusion matrix for a classification problem tells us the number of correct and incorrect predictions for every class (for example wealthy). For example, the model may have classified 10 countries with average GDP as average and classified 2 countries with average GDP incorrectly, shown in the below confusion matrix. Linear regression performed exceptionally well on countries with average GDP.

By calculating the total correct examples over all examples classified, we get an average accuracy of around 58%. Mean absolute error, or the average difference between predicted and actual GDP per capita, was $3299

Support Vector Machine: Explanation

Support Vector Machine, or SVR, is unlike other regression models in that it doesn't try to minimize the error between the real and predicted value. Instead, it tries to find the best fit line within a threshold value, the distance between the hyperplane and boundary line, and then uses that to make a prediction.

Neutral Networks: Results

Looking at the confusion matrix below, the neural network performed much better on wealthy and very wealthy nations.

For total accuracy, the NN performed at 64.4% accuracy in classification. Mean absolute error is $2311

K Nearest Neighbors: Explanation

K Nearest Neighbors looks at data points in the testing set and infers the classification based on similarity to other points of the same class in the training set.

K Nearest Neighbors: Results

Looking at our confusion matrix below, K Nearest Neighbors performed well on very wealthy and ver poor nations.

Conclusion


To summarize, we he have developed a regression AI that is able to predict a house’s value in California based on factors like age, location, total rooms, etc. We created graphs to analyze and understand our data. We utilized algorithms like K Nearest Neighbor and Random Forest Generator to sort our data with supervised machine learning.

So what?

So what is the significance of our project? Because we know which features are most correlated with a higher GDP country, we can identify both the factors that lead to countries with higher monetary value and also predict and then invest in countries with a high likelihood of future growth.
